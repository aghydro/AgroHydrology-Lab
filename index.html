Welcome to the AgroHydrology Lab

The AgroHydrology Lab at Mississippi State University focuses on developing sustainable solutions for agricultural water management and climate-resilient cropping systems. Led by Dr. Sayantan Samanta, our research integrates advanced modeling frameworks, remote sensing technologies, and field-scale experimentation to address critical challenges in agroecosystem sustainability.

We work at the intersection of hydrology, agronomy, and environmental science to quantify the impacts of agricultural management practices on water conservation, soil health, and crop productivity under changing climate conditions.

Agroecosystem Modeling

Development and application of process-based models to simulate crop growth, water dynamics, and ecosystem services at field and watershed scales.

Water Conservation

Assessment of soil and water management practices for enhancing water use efficiency and building climate resilience in agricultural systems.

Remote Sensing

Utilization of unmanned aerial systems (UAS) and satellite imagery for monitoring soil moisture, crop health, and agricultural management impacts.

Decision Support Systems

Creation of tools and frameworks to support farmers and stakeholders in making informed decisions about irrigation, tillage, and crop management.

Research Focus Areas

Agrohydrological and Agroecosystem Sustainability

Our research investigates the complex interactions between water, soil, and crops to develop sustainable agricultural systems. We assess how management practices such as conservation tillage, cover cropping, and regenerative agriculture impact hydrological processes, soil health, and ecosystem services at multiple scales.

Soil and Water Management

We quantify the effects of soil management practices on plant-available water, infiltration rates, runoff generation, and sediment loss. Our work incorporates changes in soil structure and hydrologic properties to more accurately model water conservation benefits and downstream water quality impacts.

Innovations in Agroecosystem Modeling Frameworks

The lab develops novel approaches for representing agricultural management practices in watershed-scale models. We work with tools including SWAT (Soil and Water Assessment Tool), APEX (Agricultural Policy/Environmental eXtender), and DSSAT (Decision Support System for Agrotechnology Transfer) to improve predictions of crop-water-soil interactions.

Climate Change Impacts and Adaptation

Using CMIP6 future climate projections, we evaluate how changing temperature and precipitation patterns will affect crop production, growing season length, and harvest timing across major agricultural regions. We identify climate-resilient crops and adaptation strategies to maintain productivity under future conditions.

Remote Sensing Applications in Agrohydrology

We employ UAS-based multispectral and RGB imaging combined with machine learning algorithms to estimate soil water content, assess crop stress, and monitor the effects of conservation practices. This work bridges field measurements with model predictions for improved agricultural monitoring.

Current Research Themes:
  • Regenerative agricultural practices and water quality
  • Deficit irrigation strategies for water-limited regions
  • Cover crop impacts on soil hydrology
  • Alternative crops for climate resilience
  • Watershed-scale hydrological modeling

Lab Director

Dr. Sayantan Samanta

Assistant Professor

Department: Agricultural & Biological Engineering
Education: Ph.D. in Water Management and Hydrological Science, Texas A&M University (2023)
Email: ssamanta@abe.msstate.edu

Dr. Samanta joined Mississippi State University in August 2025 as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Agricultural & Biological Engineering. His research focuses on agrohydrological sustainability, soil and water conservation, agroecosystem modeling, and remote sensing applications in agriculture. Before joining MSU, he served as a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Texas A&M AgriLife Research in Vernon, Texas, where he conducted research on sustainable agricultural intensification and climate change impacts on cotton production.

Teaching

ABE 4263/6263: Soil and Water Management (Spring Semester)

This course covers fundamental principles and practical applications of soil and water management in agricultural systems, including irrigation design, drainage, erosion control, and water conservation strategies.
